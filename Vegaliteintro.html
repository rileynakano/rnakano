What trends do you notice? What’s interesting in the data? Are there certain platforms or genres that dominate the sales?

Visualization 2: Sales Over Time by Platform and Genre

How have video game sales changed for different platforms, genres, and publishers?

Reflection:
The spread of popularairty has stayed relatively consistent; however, it can easily be seen when new consoles roll out due to the quick color shift in the graph. Before 1990, games were heavily dominated by the Atari 2600 and the NES, respectively. When the years go on, we see more competition when Xbox and PlayStation come in, while Nintendo is still producing home consoles like the Wii & Wii U, while dominating the handheld console field with the DS and 3DS. With more consoles coming out, the number of games sold has gone up as well, and we see a very consistent incline in global sales until the dip in recent years, due to how recent the data is to the game releases.

Design Decisions:
The year and sales are the x and y axes, respectively; this gives the viewer an immediate idea of how each game did and the performance over time. The platform was chosen to be color instead of genre because the platform lifespan will automatically categorize over time, grouping each platform very easily. Each shape represents a genre. If I made the platform be represented by a shape, there would be too many shapes to ever make out what is what. With fewer categories in the genre, using different shapes works much better.

Visualization 3: Regional Sales vs. Platform

Do you notice any regional preferences or patterns? Which platforms are more popular in specific regions?

Reflection:
I mostly noticed how much other areas don’t purchase games since the other regions' color is consistently at the bottom. Other than that, Europe seems to heavily prefer PlayStation consoles and PCs, while North Americans prefer the Xbox series as their console of choice. Old Nintendo consoles such as the NES and SNES were very Japan-dominated, and once the Wii came out, Nintendo started gaining a larger audience from other regions, especially North America.

Design Decisions:
The region being represented by color was a choice I made because with only 4 things that are being measured and with a lot of possible overlapping, color allows the viewer to know exactly which region is being represented. The x-axis is the platform stacked on top of this because it won’t scatter the plot at all, so the only thing that a viewer might have to think about when looking at this visualization is how many sales it had, which is okay because that is what the y-axis of a graph is for.
